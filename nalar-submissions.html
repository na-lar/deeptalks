What Can Be Submitted?

Submissions should be informational works that aim to deepen understanding of a topic, rather than purely entertain.

We're looking for material that helps participants think clearly, reason rigorously, and engage with meaningful ideas.

Accepted formats

  • Articles or essays
  • Book chapters
  • Documentaries
  • Podcasts or recorded talks
  • Lectures, interviews, or debates

Core Criteria

A good submission should:

Prioritise insight over storytelling

Analysis, argument, explanation, or investigation — not narrative for its own sake.

Be grounded in reality

Philosophy, psychology, science, society, culture — something with real-world stakes.

Offer something to think about

A perspective, argument, framework, or question that's worth discussing together.

Take between 30 minutes and 2 hours to consume

Enough depth to generate discussion, not so long it becomes a burden.

Format Requirements

  • The material must be digitally accessible for free
  • Submissions must include either a direct link to the material, or a file shared with the host

What Doesn't Fit

  • Fiction — novels, short stories, narrative films
  • Content primarily designed for entertainment
  • Highly technical material requiring specialised expertise
